Student Competition Challenges

Registrations are now closed. Event day showcases begin at 09:00

01
Innovation Challenge (Micro:bit)
Grades: 5-9
Team Size: 4 Learners
Equipment: Micro:bit provided
Design and build innovative solutions using micro:bit technology to solve real-world problems. Teams will present working prototypes that demonstrate creativity, functionality, and problem-solving skills.

Skills Developed:

  • Programming and logical thinking
  • Electronic circuit design
  • Creative problem-solving
  • Presentation and communication
02
goIT Challenge
Grades: 8-9
Team Size: 4 Learners
Focus: App Development
Registered Teams: 20 Teams
Learners identify a problem in their community, design, and build an app solution. Teams present their app prototypes on the day, demonstrating how technology can solve real-world challenges and improve community life.

Skills Developed:

  • App design and user interface development
  • Problem identification and solution design
  • Community research and needs analysis
  • Presentation and pitching skills
03
Drone Challenge
Grades: 10-12
Team Size: 4 Learners
Equipment: Drones provided
Program and pilot drones through complex obstacle courses and mission-based challenges. Teams will demonstrate precision flying, automated programming, and creative problem-solving in aerospace technology.

Skills Developed:

  • Drone programming and control
  • Spatial reasoning and navigation
  • Safety protocols and procedures
  • Aerospace engineering concepts
04
Unplugged Coding
Grades: 3-4
Team Size: 4 Learners
Format: Hands-on Activities
Learn fundamental programming concepts through engaging hands-on activities that require no computers. Teams solve puzzles, create algorithms, and demonstrate logical thinking through creative physical activities.

Skills Developed:

  • Algorithmic thinking and sequencing
  • Pattern recognition and logic
  • Collaborative problem-solving
  • Communication and instruction-giving
05
Spike Prime Challenge (FLL)
Grades: 6-10
Team Size: 4 Learners
Equipment: LEGO Spike Prime
Design, build, and program LEGO robots to complete real-world mission challenges. Teams combine engineering, programming, and research to develop innovative solutions to global problems.

Skills Developed:

  • Mechanical engineering and design
  • Robot programming and sensors
  • Research and innovation thinking
  • Project management and teamwork
